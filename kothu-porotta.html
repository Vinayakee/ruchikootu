Kothu Porotta

A spicy, smoky, and absolutely indulgent South Indian street food made with shredded porottas, masala, eggs, and optional meat — all tossed on a hot tawa for the ultimate flavor bomb!

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3
Dinner Street Food Spicy Kerala

Instructions

STEP 1

Shred leftover porottas into small pieces using hands or a knife.

STEP 2

In a large tawa or pan, heat oil. Sauté chopped onions, green chilies, and curry leaves until golden.

STEP 3

Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy.

STEP 4

Add turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the masala well.

STEP 5

Add beaten eggs (and cooked chicken or beef if using). Scramble until cooked.

STEP 6

Add shredded porotta and mix everything together. Use two spatulas to chop and mix. Serve hot with raita or curry.

Ingredients

  • 4 porottas, shredded
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, sliced
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup cooked chicken/beef (optional)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste

Tools

  • Flat tawa or large frying pan
  • Wooden spatulas (preferably two)
  • Knife or scissors (to shred porottas)
  • Mixing bowl
