Kerala Fish Curry

A spicy and tangy traditional Kerala-style fish curry, simmered in a clay pot with tamarind, coconut, and fragrant spices. Best enjoyed with steamed rice or kappa (tapioca).

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 25 mins
Servings: 4
Lunch Seafood Kerala Spicy

Instructions

STEP 1

Clean and cut the fish into medium-sized pieces. Soak tamarind in warm water and extract thick pulp.

STEP 2

Heat coconut oil in a clay pot. Add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.

STEP 3

Add chopped shallots, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until golden. Then add turmeric, chili, and coriander powder. Sauté well.

STEP 4

Add tamarind extract and salt. Let it boil for 5–7 minutes to blend the flavors.

STEP 5

Add fish pieces carefully. Cover and cook on low flame for 10–15 minutes or until fish is cooked through. Do not stir too much.

STEP 6

Finish with a drizzle of coconut oil and a few fresh curry leaves. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish (seer, sardines, or pomfret)
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 10 shallots, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1-inch ginger, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1½ tbsp coriander powder
  • Small lemon-sized tamarind
  • Salt to taste
  • 1½ cups water

Tools

  • Clay pot (manchatti) or deep pan
  • Ladle
  • Small bowl for tamarind
  • Knife and chopping board
