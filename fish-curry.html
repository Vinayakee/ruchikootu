Kerala Fish Curry

Bold, tangy, and packed with coastal flavor, Kerala Fish Curry is a spicy delight made with tamarind, coconut, and freshly ground spices. A staple in every Malayali home!

Prep Time: 20 mins
Cook Time: 30 mins
Servings: 4
Lunch Non-Vegetarian Spicy Kerala

Instructions

STEP 1

Clean and cut fish into medium pieces. Marinate with turmeric and salt. Set aside for 15–20 mins.

STEP 2

In a clay pot or pan, heat coconut oil and sauté fenugreek, mustard seeds, and curry leaves.

STEP 3

Add shallots, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until soft. Mix in chili powder, coriander, and turmeric. Sauté on low flame.

STEP 4

Pour tamarind water and let it boil. Add fish pieces carefully. Cover and simmer for 15 minutes.

STEP 5

Once the fish is cooked and curry thickens, drizzle raw coconut oil on top and garnish with curry leaves. Serve hot with rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish (seer, sardine, or mackerel)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind (soaked in warm water)
  • 6 shallots, sliced
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil

Tools

  • Clay pot (manchatti) or deep pan
  • Spatula
  • Mixing bowls
  • Knife and chopping board
