Kerala Fish Curry

<<<<<<< Updated upstream

Bold, tangy, and packed with coastal flavor, Kerala Fish Curry is a spicy delight made with tamarind, coconut, and freshly ground spices. A staple in every Malayali home!

Prep Time: 20 mins
Cook Time: 30 mins
=======

A spicy and tangy traditional Kerala-style fish curry, simmered in a clay pot with tamarind, coconut, and fragrant spices. Best enjoyed with steamed rice or kappa (tapioca).

Instructions

STEP 1
<<<<<<< Updated upstream

Clean and cut fish into medium pieces. Marinate with turmeric and salt. Set aside for 15–20 mins.

STEP 2

In a clay pot or pan, heat coconut oil and sauté fenugreek, mustard seeds, and curry leaves.

STEP 3

Add shallots, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until soft. Mix in chili powder, coriander, and turmeric. Sauté on low flame.

STEP 4

Pour tamarind water and let it boil. Add fish pieces carefully. Cover and simmer for 15 minutes.

STEP 5

Once the fish is cooked and curry thickens, drizzle raw coconut oil on top and garnish with curry leaves. Serve hot with rice.

=======

Clean and cut the fish into medium-sized pieces. Soak tamarind in warm water and extract thick pulp.

STEP 2

Heat coconut oil in a clay pot. Add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.

STEP 3

Add chopped shallots, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until golden. Then add turmeric, chili, and coriander powder. Sauté well.

STEP 4

Add tamarind extract and salt. Let it boil for 5–7 minutes to blend the flavors.

STEP 5

Add fish pieces carefully. Cover and cook on low flame for 10–15 minutes or until fish is cooked through. Do not stir too much.

STEP 6

Finish with a drizzle of coconut oil and a few fresh curry leaves. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes before serving.
