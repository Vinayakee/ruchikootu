Rasam

A tangy, spiced South Indian soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and a blend of spices. It's light, flavorful, and perfect as a digestive after a hearty meal.

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 15 mins
Servings: 4
Vegetarian Kerala Sadhya Digestive

Instructions

STEP 1

Soak tamarind in warm water and extract juice. Set aside.

STEP 2

Crush black pepper, cumin, and garlic coarsely. Heat a pan with a little oil, sauté crushed mixture and curry leaves.

STEP 3

Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, and sauté till soft. Pour in tamarind extract, salt, and water. Let it simmer gently for 8–10 minutes.

STEP 4

Once aromatic, sprinkle chopped coriander leaves. In a small pan, splutter mustard seeds and red chilies in ghee. Add this tempering to the rasam and switch off the flame.

STEP 5

Serve hot as a soup or over rice. A comforting and flavorful addition to any Kerala meal!

Ingredients

  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 4–5 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tsp ghee or oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • Curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 2–3 cups water

Tools

  • Small saucepan
  • Tempering ladle
  • Wooden spatula
  • Strainer (optional)
  • Measuring spoons
