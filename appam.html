Appam

Appam is a soft and lacy rice pancake, a traditional Kerala delicacy often paired with coconut milk-based stews or spicy curries. Its delicate texture and mild flavor make it a breakfast favorite.

Instructions

STEP 1
<<<<<<< Updated upstream

Soak raw rice for 4-5 hours. Grind with grated coconut and cooked rice into a smooth batter using water as needed.

STEP 2

Add yeast, sugar, and salt. Mix well and allow the batter to ferment overnight or for 8 hours.

STEP 3

Once fermented, stir the batter gently. Adjust consistency if too thick using a little water.

STEP 4

Heat an appachatti (appam pan), pour a ladle of batter, swirl to spread, and cover. Cook until edges crisp and center is soft.

STEP 5

Serve hot with vegetable stew, egg curry, or fish molee!

Soak raw rice and urad dal for 4-5 hours. Drain and grind to a smooth batter along with cooked rice and grated coconut.

STEP 2

Add yeast dissolved in warm water with a little sugar to the batter. Mix well and ferment overnight.

STEP 3

Add salt and a little sugar the next morning. The batter should be of pouring consistency but not too runny.

STEP 4

Heat an appachatti (appam pan), pour a ladle of batter, swirl the pan to spread thin edges and thick center. Cover and cook until soft and lacy.

STEP 5

Serve hot with vegetable stew, chicken curry, or sweetened coconut milk.
