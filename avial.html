Avial

Ideal vegetable dish which goes best with hot rice. An extravagant treat for vegetable lovers.

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 30 mins
Servings: 4
Lunch Homely Vegetarian Fresh

Instructions

STEP 1

Grind coconut, green chilli, cumin seeds, and shallot to a slightly coarse paste without water. Set aside.

STEP 2

Place all vegetables in a thick-bottomed pan. Add ½ tsp turmeric powder, mix, and add water. Press veggies to ensure they're immersed.

STEP 3

Cover and boil. Simmer for 10 minutes till vegetables are half cooked.

STEP 4

Add raw mango and salt. Mix well, cover again and cook for 10 more minutes or until fully cooked.

STEP 5

Add the ground paste, remaining 1 tsp turmeric powder, and tamarind pulp. Cook for 5 more minutes.

STEP 6

Heat oil in a pan, crackle mustard seeds and curry leaves, and pour this seasoning over the aviyal. Mix gently.

STEP 7

Serve hot with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup Raw Banana (Vazhakai), cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Brinjal (Kathirikai), cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Drumstick (Murungaikai), cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Potato, cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Carrot, cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Elephant Yam (Chena Kizhangu), cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Cucumber, cut lengthwise
  • 5 String Beans, cut lengthwise
  • 5 Cluster Beans (Kothavarangai), cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup White Pumpkin, cut lengthwise
  • ½ cup Snake Gourd (Pudalangai), cut lengthwise
  • 1.5 tsp Turmeric Powder (Manjal Podi)
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ to ¾ cup Water
  • ½ cup Sour Raw Mango
  • 2 tsp Tamarind Pulp
  • 2 cups Grated Coconut
  • 4 to 5 Green Chillies
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeerakam)
  • 1 Shallot (Sambar Onion/Chinna Ulli)
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds (Kaduku)
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves

Tools

  • Heavy-bottomed pan
  • Mixing spoon
  • Small tempering pan
  • Blender or grinder
