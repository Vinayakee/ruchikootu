Paayasam

A traditional South Indian dessert made during festivals and celebrations — creamy, rich, and flavored with cardamom, cashews, and ghee.

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 40 mins
Servings: 5
Dessert Festive Kerala Vegetarian

Instructions

STEP 1

Wash and soak rice (or vermicelli, moong dal depending on variety) for 15 mins if needed.

STEP 2

In a heavy-bottomed pan, cook the rice in water or a little milk until soft and mushy.

STEP 3

Add jaggery (or sugar) and stir until fully dissolved. Let it simmer and thicken slightly.

STEP 4

Add cardamom powder and coconut milk or cow’s milk. Stir and let it blend well. Do not boil after adding thick coconut milk.

STEP 5

Fry cashews, raisins, and a pinch of dry ginger (optional) in ghee. Add to the paayasam and serve warm or chilled.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup broken matta rice (or vermicelli, moong dal)
  • 3/4 cup jaggery (or 1/2 cup sugar)
  • 2 cups thin coconut milk
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 10 cashews
  • 10 raisins
  • Pinch of dry ginger powder (optional)
  • Water as needed

Tools

  • Heavy-bottomed pan or uruli
  • Ladle or wooden spoon
  • Strainer
  • Measuring cups/spoons
  • Small frying pan
