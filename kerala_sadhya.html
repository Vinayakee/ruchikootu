Kerala Sadhya

A grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf, Kerala Sadhya features over 20 dishes including rice, curries, pickles, and desserts. It’s a celebration of flavor, tradition, and togetherness.

Prep Time: 2 hours
Cook Time: 1.5 hours
Servings: 4–6
Festival Vegetarian Traditional Kerala

Instructions

STEP 1

Plan the menu: Rice, Sambar, Parippu, Avial, Thoran, Kaalan, Olan, Pachadi, Kichadi, Inji curry, Pickle, Pappadam, and Payasam.

STEP 2

Cook parboiled rice and prepare each curry with coconut, vegetables, and spices. Use fresh grated coconut wherever required.

STEP 3

Fry pappadams, prepare chutneys, and boil payasam ingredients slowly for rich flavor.

STEP 4

Assemble everything fresh and warm on a banana leaf. Start with salt, pickles, pachadi, kichadi, and end with payasam.

Key Dishes & Ingredients

  • Rice – parboiled Kerala rice
  • Parippu – moong dal, coconut, cumin
  • Sambar – mixed vegetables, toor dal, sambar powder
  • Avial – mixed vegetables, coconut, curd
  • Olan – ash gourd, red beans, coconut milk
  • Kaalan – raw banana, yam, curd, pepper
  • Thoran – cabbage/beans with coconut
  • Pachadi – beetroot or pineapple with curd
  • Kichadi – cucumber/yam with curd
  • Pickles – mango, lemon, ginger
  • Pappadam – fried and crispy
  • Payasam – Ada pradhaman or Semiya

Tools

  • Banana leaves for serving
  • Pressure cooker
  • Large pots for multiple dishes
  • Grater and coconut scraper
  • Serving ladles and pans
