Malabar Biryani

One of Kerala‚Äôs iconic dishes, Malabar Biryani is known for its rich aroma, flavorful meat masala, and fragrant jeerakasala rice. A perfect centerpiece for any celebration!

Prep Time: 40 mins
Cook Time: 45 mins
Servings: 5
Main Course Non-Vegetarian Malabar Special

Instructions

üî∏ Prepare Rice

STEP 1

Wash and soak jeerakasala rice for 20 minutes. Cook with salt, ghee, cloves, cinnamon, and cardamom until 90% done. Drain and keep aside.

üî∏ Make Chicken Masala

STEP 2

Marinate chicken with yogurt, chili powder, turmeric, coriander, and garam masala. Let it rest for 30 minutes.

STEP 3

Heat ghee and oil in a pan. Saut√© onions until golden brown. Add green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and cook until soft.

STEP 4

Add marinated chicken. Cook until chicken is tender and masala is thick. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves.

üî∏ Layering and Dum

STEP 5

In a heavy-bottomed pan, layer rice and chicken masala alternately. Top with fried onions, cashews, raisins, ghee, and saffron milk.

STEP 6

Cover tightly and place on dum (low flame or in oven) for 20 minutes. Gently mix before serving.

Ingredients

üîπ For Rice

  • 2 cups jeerakasala rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • 3 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1 small cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

üîπ For Chicken Masala

  • 750g chicken, cleaned and cut
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tsp chili powder, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint and coriander leaves
  • 2 tbsp ghee + 2 tbsp oil

üîπ For Garnish

  • Fried onions
  • Fried cashews and raisins
  • 2 tbsp saffron milk

Tools

  • Heavy-bottomed biryani pot
  • Rice cooker or pot
  • Deep pan for chicken
  • Measuring cups
